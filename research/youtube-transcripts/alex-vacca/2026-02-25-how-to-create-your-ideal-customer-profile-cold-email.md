## Key takeaways
- Split one ICP into three buying-behavior segments (Alex's example: funded SaaS startups closing in ~2 weeks, D2C brands on Shopify with 20-200k Instagram followers, and enterprise 500+ employee accounts with 3-6 month sales cycles) instead of blasting one 10,000-person list.
- Layer ATL (C-suite, VP, director — ROI, competitive advantage) vs BTL (managers, users — features, workflow) personas at roughly a 1:2 to 1:4 ratio (1:5 for content teams, 1:2 for sales), yielding six unique targeting combinations from one ICP.
- Segment prospects across three layers: firmographics (employee count, ARR, funding year, tech stack), sales triggers (hiring content writers = content investment, hiring RevOps = messy data, recent funding = budget), and probing intent questions like "what does your current outbound process look like from start to finish?"
- Run three copy variations per persona (18 total) testing subject lines, CTAs (e.g., "worth a 15-minute call" vs "can I send over some times"), and value props (time savings vs money savings) to find message-market fit.
- Test by volume, not time — send to 1,000-1,500 contacts per segment split ATL/BTL, let the first two sequence steps run ~6 days, and expect message-market fit in 1-3 campaigns (max 4 for complex offers).
- Same product, different emails: for a video-editing tool, the D2C director of marketing gets "your content team's producing 3x more" focused on ROI, while the social media manager gets "stop spending forever editing one Instagram reel" focused on getting home on time.
