## Key takeaways
- ColdIQ built four internal agents (ICP definition, offer packaging, list building, copywriting) trained on 3,000+ real outbound campaigns and 250+ clients, run alongside "cold code" to execute the campaign end-to-end.
- Their ICP agent uses a six-factor weighted scoring model — product-market fit 30%, outbound readiness 25%, value-prop clarity 15%, TAM size 15%, multi-channel willingness 10%, sector fit 5% — bucketing leads into tier 1 (>2.5), tier 2 (1.5-2.4), tier 3 (<1.5).
- Tier-one ColdIQ prospects are B2B SaaS/fintech, 20-200 employees, $2M-$20M ARR, Series A/B, in US/UK/Canada/Australia, with 10,000+ TAM; personas include VP Sales, CRO, founder/CEO, head of growth, RevOps lead, SDR manager.
- The list-builder prompt from the ICP agent is pasted into ColdIQ's internal builder (filtering on job title, "in position <1 year," posts on LinkedIn, seniority, revenue), pushed to a Clay table, exported as CSV, and re-uploaded to cold code for enrichment and tiering.
- Cold code found emails, split 54 leads into 20 tier-1 / 12 tier-2 / 22 tier-3, then wrote three-email sequences referencing specifics like "CoreView at 120 people post series B with Marketo, HubSpot, and Drift" and auto-pushed the first three leads into an Instantly campaign.
- Alex's punchline: precision beats volume — "people who send 200 emails and book 15 meetings" win over people sending 10,000, and this exact system took ColdIQ from zero to $350K/month.
