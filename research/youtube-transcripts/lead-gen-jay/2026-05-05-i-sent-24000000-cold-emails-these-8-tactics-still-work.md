## Key takeaways
- Filtering Apollo lists down to only CEOs, founders, CMOs, CFOs, CROs, owners, and presidents (dropping managers, coordinators, and "specialist" titles) doubles reply rates because decision makers either reply or ignore, whereas mid-managers forward or delete.
- Jay's "spam folder mining" play - pulling cold emails that landed in his spam, diagnosing the deliverability issue, and replying "your cold email landed in my spam yesterday, here's why, here's the fix, want help?" - returns 20-30% reply rates vs. 1% on normal blasts.
- Reverse lead magnets built with Consulti + Lovable/Bolt averaged 3-5% reply rates and hit 7%+ on top campaigns; one dev-tool campaign scraped ~23,000 GitHub open-source contributors, generated custom landing pages per handle, and got 300+ replies with 70 qualified opportunities at ~4%.
- For sending infrastructure, run all Google under 100 mailboxes, but at 500+ split roughly half Google with the remainder across Microsoft and private SMTP because Google's November deliverability update dropped industry inbox placement from 80-90% to 50% in 72 hours.
- Kill Calendly links, open tracking, link tracking, images, and trigger words in email one, and always add spin tax vetted for blacklisted phrases (Jay recommends Consulti's spin tax generator over Apollo/Instantly's native versions).
- Use the "triple tap" copy framework: subject line + first sentence must not telegraph the sale, keep body to ~4 sentences (why you, specific pain, casual social proof, ask), and the CTA must be answerable with a one-word "yes" or "sure" - no calendar links or opt-in forms.
