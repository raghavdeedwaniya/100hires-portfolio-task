## Key takeaways
- Cap sequences at max 3 emails (Eric prefers 2); size by TAM — 1M+ contacts = 1 email then reset with new insight, 50-100K = 2 emails, under 50K = 3 emails.
- Space follow-ups 3-5 days apart, then re-hit the same TAM as a fresh campaign 1-3 months later, since business changes (not follow-up frequency) create new buying windows.
- Flip the value-prop angle in email 2 across the core levers (save time, save money, make more money, reduce risk) — if email 1 pitched saving time, email 2 pitches making more money.
- Lower the ask in the follow-up: go for the SQL/meeting in email 1, then offer a lead magnet or hand-raiser resource (MQL) in email 2 or 3.
- Try Kyle Rasmusen's two-step "building it for you" sequence: email 1 says "I'll build a report for you," then email 2 actually delivers the AI-generated asset (Eric tested this on a customer and it worked really well).
- Namedrop other relevant people at the account (e.g., pull the CEO or director of sales when emailing the VP of Sales) using Prospeo, Clay, or the Blitz API endpoint that returns all employees with titles in one call.
