## Key takeaways
- Keep subject lines to 2-4 words max, and A/B test proper case vs. sentence case vs. all lowercase — no emojis, exclamation points, or multiple question marks.
- Mimic subject lines a colleague or customer would send (e.g., for a pizza-restaurant client Eric used the subject "pizza orders" paired with preview text "Hey [first name], I tried calling [phone number] but figured email would be easier").
- Still test "quick question" and "intro" as control subject lines against any new ideas — Eric says they consistently work despite being hated on.
- Optimize the subject line plus preview text as a unit for the mobile push notification and lock-screen view, using the subject to open a loop and the preview to deepen it.
- Use the competitor-engagement play when possible: subject = "[Company name] competitors", preview = "I have a list of people engaging with competitor" — a real campaign Eric runs for Growth Engine X.
- If personalizing with variables like company name, normalize the subject line so casing/formatting doesn't look machine-generated.
