## Key takeaways
- Prospects lie to pitchers the same way Josh lied to the Hugo Boss associate ("no thanks, I'm just looking") because they know engaging will trigger a sales push.
- Ditch pitch openers like "the reason for my call is we've discovered a breakthrough in automating payouts, what we do is X" — shift from statements to a question that illuminates a potential problem without leading to a desired answer.
- Example open for CaptivateIQ: "How are you currently giving reps access to how their commissions are paid — is it something they can see at any time or are you sending statements on a one-off basis?" then shut the front door and listen.
- Presuppose they've already considered your category ("you've been doing this for a while, you've probably considered automating it") and if they confirm, follow with "sounds like there's a reason you didn't switch" and listen.
- Come from genuine curiosity and let go of assumptions — "buyers have the answers, sellers have the questions. Ditch the pitch, poke the bear."
