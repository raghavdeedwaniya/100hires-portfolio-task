## Key takeaways
- Skip the traditional guru response ("you don't need to think about it, all you need is more information") — prospects feel the push and pull away.
- Stop "handling" the objection at all; drop back, pause, and use elicitation instead.
- Elicitation = take an educated guess about the other person and let them confirm or correct you (e.g., the Hugo Boss salesperson's "business trip" guess triggered Josh's reflex to correct him with "no, actually, I'm going to a wedding").
- Applied to "I want to think about it" on a car sale: guess "you probably thought the payment was going to be a few hundred less a month," then shut the front door and listen — the prospect will correct you with the real number ("I thought it was going to be 75 less").
- The payoff chain: "Without truth, there's no trust. Without trust, there's no transaction."
