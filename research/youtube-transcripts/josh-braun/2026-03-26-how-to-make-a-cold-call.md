## Key takeaways
- Assume the prospect already has a solution ("car wash bucket") and shine a light on a potential problem with it instead of pitching a replacement.
- Frame the problem tentatively without assuming they have it (e.g., "not sure if this is happening with you, but... dirt can settle to the bottom of the bucket and scratch the hood") then ask how they prevent it.
- After asking the question, "shut the front door and listen" — silence forces the prospect to reveal whether it's a fit or not.
- If they've heard of your category (the "grit guard"), take a guess at why they aren't using it ("sounds like you used it, but it didn't really work out") to draw out the real objection.
- Operating principle: "It's not your job to fill people's heads with information when you cold call. Your job is to draw it out. Buyers have the answers. Sellers have the questions."
