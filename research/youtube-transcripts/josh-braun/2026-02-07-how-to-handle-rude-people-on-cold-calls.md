## Key takeaways
- Accept upfront that "a world without rude or angry people isn't possible" — don't beg, convince, take a knee, or apologize for interrupting.
- Change your intent: if your goal is to be liked by everyone, every rude person will crush you; lower expectations and detach from the outcome.
- Respond with neutral energy — "not cold, not unfriendly, just neutral" — pause, take a breath, and lean back before answering.
- When a prospect pushes back on price ("we can get sales trainers for far less than what you're charging"), simply say "you can" and let the silence sit.
- When someone says "F you, never call here again," just say "Okay," hang up, and call the next person — you control your energy and peace, not their reaction.
- Reframe the job: "It's not your job to be liked by everyone. Your job is to illuminate a potential problem and stay grounded."
