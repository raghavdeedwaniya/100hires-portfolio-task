## Key takeaways
- Open every call with a permission-based signal opener like "totally a cold call, but promise it's well researched — do you have 15 seconds I can tell you why I'm reaching out?" — it earned Alex his one meeting of the hour.
- Stack multiple relevance signals up front (podcast host, LinkedIn follower, downloaded content) — Alex named three signals in 30 seconds to disarm the meeting-book prospect.
- To beat voicemail gatekeepers/phone screeners, lead with a concrete signal ("a ton of folks on your team are downloading our content") rather than just a name — Alex got past a screener that way.
- Verify data before dialing: one prospect hung up because "we fix crash sheets" and another said "not where I work" — bad data burns the call before it starts.
- Know when to disengage — Alex kept a talkative prospect on the line even after realizing within 3 minutes the buyer had budget and solution locked in, framing it as a conscious tradeoff (relationship vs. dial volume).
- Final tally from the live hour: 26 dials, 5 connects (~19% connect rate), 1 meeting booked (~20% meeting-set rate on connects) — realistic baseline for one hour on a fresh list.
