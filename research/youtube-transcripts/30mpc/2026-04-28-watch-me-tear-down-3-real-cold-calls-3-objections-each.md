## Key takeaways
- Avoid the "double clutch" (asking "how are you?" or "is this [name]?") — assume it's the right person and let them correct you, so you don't burn the first 40 seconds of the call.
- Run a "reverse pitch": instead of describing the product, pitch a problem a similar customer had ("directors of safety are measured on reducing incidences, but they can't tell if reps actually know the safety guidelines") to pull the buyer into your world.
- When a prospect gets emotional (e.g., "you said LinkedIn but my cell isn't on there"), acknowledge before handling — "sure, I can send an email, sounds like I caught you on a job site" — because they're objecting to the emotion, not the content.
- Multi-thread early on booked meetings by inviting a payroll or IT counterpart to the first call; the reviewer flagged this HRIS rep for pulling in a Paycor primary and IT owner right at scheduling.
- Convert time zones for the prospect when proposing meeting times (e.g., translate Pacific into their Central) — a small executive-assistant-level detail most reps skip.
- Disqualify by job title before dialing — the DevOps rep called a "renewable engineering" leader who was the wrong type of engineer entirely, which no objection handling could rescue.
