## Key takeaways
- If reading the email aloud feels like a tongue twister or you need a breath mid-sentence, the sentence is too long — target under 100 words (the winning rewrite landed at 139 words, down from 198).
- Skip generic subject lines that include your prospect's company name (e.g., "partnership with Acme Inc") — instead drop a specific project name pulled from their website, since "every single company brags about the projects they work on."
- Dollar-figure social proof like "cut $500,000 in hiring costs" fails without context — the buyer can't tell if that's meaningful for their company size, so anchor numbers to a comparable peer.
- The winning email (SheShed) opened with genuine research — a custom cartoon plus a specific founder quote about TikTok ads — proving personalization on a real story beats AI-generated flattery like "that's the kind of story that belongs in a business school case study."
- When rewriting, name the specific competitor being displaced (e.g., "solutions like Shopify Capital take a percentage of your sales and don't let you apply funds outside of Shopify") so the differentiator is concrete.
- Cut ChatGPT tells like the "yada yada, here's the quote" pattern and positive-reinforcement filler sentences that "don't sound like how a person would talk."
