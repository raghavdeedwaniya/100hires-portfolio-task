## Key takeaways
- Armand's SDR turnaround playbook was "flip two, fire two, hire two": promote two young guns to hit a $200/week dial minimum, cut two culture-drag underperformers, and hire two hungry replacements to reset momentum fast.
- Hire for career changers or fresh grads who show propensity to learn/write (teachers, screenwriters, athletes, debate, VC interns like Andreessen Horowitz) — of his top 5 SDRs ever hired, zero had prior SDR experience.
- Interview process was mock cold call on the second conversation, then a super-round of mock cold email, a targeting/brain-teaser exercise, and a rep "beer test" — run two mock calls back-to-back to see if the candidate implements feedback between call 1 and call 2 (no change = gone in 3 months).
- Use "shuttle runs" for account research: have SDRs bring back only 5 accounts and 3–5 contacts each for approval before moving forward, so they don't stack 17 mistakes on a batch of 10.
- Ran a 9–10am daily dial blitz posted in the global sales Slack channel (SF vs. Salt Lake, AE vs. SDR) where every booked meeting was posted with company, AE tag, and meeting date to make SDRs publicly celebrated.
- For events like Dreamforce and HR Transform, "the money is 100% made before the event" — require reps to have 30 meetings booked before flying out, tracked weekly on a "Road to [event]" board, and cancel trips if they don't hit the number.
- Monthly "Exec Shark Tank Week" at Pave: SDRs mined execs' first-degree LinkedIn connections, ghostwrote intro requests, and extracted 10–20 warm intros/month from the executive team.
