## Key takeaways
- Elric cites Winning by Design data showing prospecting conversion has collapsed — 100 emails per opportunity in 2015, 200 in 2018, 1,000 in 2023 — while US SDR salaries doubled between 2015-2023, so cost-per-meeting is up sharply and pickup rates in the US sit near 5% (below 15% even in France).
- Despite mass layoff headlines, the global SDR count grew ~20% year-over-year; Insight Partners' Jeremy Donovan surveyed 150 portfolio companies and the top-performing GTM teams (Snowflake with ~300 SDRs vs AWS/BigQuery/Databricks) invested more in SDRs, not less.
- Don't hire junior SDRs before you have a repeatable playbook — Elric's trigger for hiring less-experienced reps is when your first hire has averaged ~12 meetings/month for 3 consecutive months; as a benchmark, Owner in the US hits 40 meetings/month per BDR with a 25% close rate, generating $70-80K sourced revenue per BDR per month.
- Segment ICP by 10-15 attributes (Snowflake reportedly uses 140), not just industry and size — at Chili Piper the qualifying stack was SaaS + go-to-market team + Salesforce, and adding a HubSpot integration multiplied TAM ~10x.
- Match message to segment: SMB restaurant buyers ($10K ACV) respond to automation-volume angles, while enterprise buyers respond to "fear of missing a Google-sized deal"; and let cold-call specialists cold call while AI handles the LinkedIn/email channels (Elric is already 40+ prompt iterations deep automating email/LinkedIn for a client with 4,000 existing customers, feeding CRM history so the AI doesn't cold-pitch known accounts).
- Test data vendors empirically — Elric ran 3 different data providers at Chili Piper because coverage varies by geography; measure both match rate and phone accuracy per ICP, and don't trust AI research (Perplexity was only "average" on his three criteria: source depth, breadth, and hallucination rate).
