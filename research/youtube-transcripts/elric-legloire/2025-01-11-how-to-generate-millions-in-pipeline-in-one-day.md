## Key takeaways
- Ralph runs prospecting days roughly every 6 weeks at Kahua (about 9 per year, all scheduled in January so reps can't claim travel conflicts), a cadence he brought from ServiceNow (2015-2019) and Tray, structured as a 15-min kickoff at 8:30-9:00, four ~90-min prospecting rounds with short breaks, a mid-day team check-in, and a wrap/happy hour.
- Each prospecting day is assigned a theme by a "tiger team" — high-value target accounts, dormant leads, closed-lost re-engagement, partner ecosystem, or a social-media push — chosen based on where the biggest gap in the current pipeline sits.
- Results at Kahua: 30-35% of the ~100 target accounts get meaningful conversations in a single day (5-10x that scale at ServiceNow), small teams average 80-100 real engaged calls (not hangups), and Ralph has seen opportunities sourced on prospecting day close within 30 days.
- Everyone plays a role, not just SDRs — execs broker warm intros into target accounts they know, SEs pre-schedule demos and discos, CS reps run adoption/usage check-ins, and partners get outreach; each function gets a prescriptive "recipe" in a dedicated Slack/Teams channel so cynics outside sales know exactly what to do.
- Reps prep several days ahead by emailing target prospects a preemptive note ("I'll call you Thursday between 8:30-9:30, 5-minute call, three topics"), and every prospecting day gets its own Salesforce dashboard so sourced pipeline can be tracked and reported up.
- Gamify to sustain energy — Ralph has run a dunk-tank where reps who hit meaningful-conversation or opportunity thresholds get to dunk the exec sponsor, plus gift cards, restaurant experiences, airline credit, and 1-800-Flowers on the company dime; and always run a postmortem after each day to fix communication and prescription gaps for the next one.
