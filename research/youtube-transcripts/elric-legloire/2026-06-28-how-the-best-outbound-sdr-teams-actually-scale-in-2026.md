## Key takeaways
- Florin argues most VC-backed teams size their SDR team top-down from a revenue goal (e.g., $10M ARR → 50 deals → 200 opps → 8 SDRs) without stress-testing the true SAM — at Common Room they found only ~6,000 of 30,000 "software companies over 50 employees" were actually serviceable, which completely reset the SDR headcount math.
- Don't hire an SDR before $1M ARR or before founders have closed the first 30-50 deals themselves — at Loopio the founders closed 50 deals before Florin was hired as the first SDR, then a second SDR was added quickly to A/B test whether failures are the person or the process.
- Florin's hiring rubric prioritizes curiosity (vetted through a homework case study: find 2 accounts, 2 contacts each, write emails, send a Loom, do a live cold call — and grade the thinking, not the output), plus a "chip on the shoulder" and a documented history of success in sports, school, or competitive gaming.
- To measure passion or coachability, skip the direct question and observe body language: ask a candidate to explain a hobby they love, watch their eyes and posture, then ask "why do you love sales" and compare — and in mock cold calls, give feedback mid-interview and see if they ask to run it again immediately.
- In 2026 the leverage is agentic signal-based selling that combines third-party intent with first-party product usage (e.g., a job-change signal enriched with "logged into your platform 10 times in the last 3 months at their old company") plus AI-run ICP scoring across 30,000 accounts in Clay or GTM Studio.
- Ramp AI-driven rep training with 100+ Hyperbound-style simulations before a new SDR's first live dial — Florin cites Owner and JumpCloud requiring reps to complete ~100 scenario role-plays before picking up the phone, resulting in faster ramp than learning by burning real prospects.
