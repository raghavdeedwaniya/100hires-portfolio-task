## Key takeaways
- Will inherited 4 SDRs with no manager who had generated only $200K pipeline in 4 months across the whole US; within ~30 days he shrunk territories to 1-3 states per rep (starting with NY, TX, CA, then PA/MI/IL), consolidated everyone onto one Gong Engage sequence, and aligned SDRs to a 1:2 or 1:3 AE ratio, pushing weekly pipeline to ~$600K with the same four reps.
- The Teachtown team hits a 92.7% show rate and 88% meeting-to-opportunity conversion by ending every cold-booked call with a live recap of the pain, then asking the prospect to accept a blank calendar invite on the phone — plus a day-before and 10-minute-before Google Calendar reminder.
- 50% of each SDR's comp is tied to the opportunity reaching stage 3 (AEs own the stage move, gated by 6 qualification bullets), which drives SDRs to keep helping after the disco — including handing over Gong recordings, BANT notes, and dollar-amount estimates to the AE.
- Will almost exclusively hires former teachers (13 of 14 reps) because they can credibly relate to special-ed directors; his signature interview question is "sell me on a passion of yours" to see whether the candidate naturally asks questions and makes it a conversation instead of pitching.
- New-hire onboarding requires shadowing 3 personalized-email research sessions and 3-4 live cold-call sessions with a paired buddy in the first two weeks, plus listening to and leaving notes on all ~45 recorded "set" calls in a shared folder.
- Will refuses AI-written outreach or AI dialers (an AI inbound SDR once agreed to demo a lawyer) and uses Gemini strictly for research — pulling district strategic plans and YouTube school-board meetings, then asking Gemini to summarize how special ed shows up in them to fuel personalized emails and calls.
