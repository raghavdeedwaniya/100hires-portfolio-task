# Manual LinkedIn collection — instructions for you

LinkedIn blocks automated scraping and login. Everything in this folder needs to come from you. Here's the exact workflow.

## For each of the 10 experts

1. Open the LinkedIn profile URL (list below).
2. Click the **Activity** tab → **Posts**.
3. Scroll and skim posts from the last **3–6 months**. Ignore comments and reshares — original posts only.
4. Pick **5–10 strongest posts** on cold email / cold calling / outbound systems / list building / deliverability. Quality over quantity — one post with a specific framework or number beats three motivational posts.
5. For each pick, copy: **post permalink URL, publish date, and the full post text.**
6. Paste into `research/linkedin-posts/_inbox.md` (create it if it doesn't exist) using the block format below, or paste them to me in chat and I'll file them.

## Block format for `_inbox.md`
```
=== <author-slug> ===
URL: <permalink>
DATE: YYYY-MM-DD
WHY: <one line — the specific tactic/framework/insight>

<full post text>

===
```

You can queue up as many blocks as you like — I'll parse them, clean the formatting, populate the per-author file, and remove them from `_inbox.md` when done.

## What "on-topic" looks like

Include:
- Cold email teardowns / frameworks (BAR, "mouse trap," reply structures)
- Specific cold call openers, objection handling scripts
- Deliverability, domain setup, warm-up numbers
- Signal-based outbound (Clay workflows, hiring signals, ICP triggers)
- LinkedIn outbound patterns
- Sequencing / cadence design
- Metrics posts (reply rates, meeting math, conversion benchmarks)

Skip:
- Motivational / career / general sales-mindset posts
- Product announcements without insight
- Reshares and comments
- Non-outbound topics (management, hiring rants, etc.) unless they specifically discuss outbound team ops
